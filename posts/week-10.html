Review

My final visual essay, which is 1 minute and 33 seconds in length, aligns closely with the statement of intent developed in week 2. It responds to the overarching question, “What is work in a digital world?” by highlighting a less visible form of digital labour: the tracking and monetisation of user behaviour. The essay suggests that everyday online actions are not passive, but instead contribute to systems that generate profit for marketing and advertising industries. Many internet users may not fully recognise that by selecting the convenient option of “accept” on cookie pop-ups, they are consenting to the collection of their data, which is then transformed into economic value.

To put the visual essay together, I used Adobe Premiere Pro, a tool which I had never used before but was optimistic about learning to use. Video clips came from Pexels and I used my phone to record my voice, talking through the script, and placed the audio on top of the video clip montage.

It is also important to note that I chose to code this blog myself. Through my time on the Web & UX course i have developed a love for coding and building the designs that I create. I also chose to learn to code in order to differentiate myself from other designers. On my portfolio I state that I am a designer who builds, branching the gap between UX design and web development. This website uses HTML for content, CSS for styling, and JavaScript for animations.

Overall, I am content with how both the visual essay and the blog were finalised. This blog website was a great way for me to display my skills in front-end development and the visual essay brought forward a compelling narrative, encouraging an audience to reconsider their digital consent choices.

If I had the opportunity to make more iterations and further develop this project, I would've liked to gamify the experience with my own cookies pop-up on this website. This idea would be beneficial to not just entertain users, but also reshape how they feel about using cookies pop-ups, in a non-harmful way. To further develop the visual essay, I would've liked to incorporate interviews to include first-hand insight on what the majority of web users understand about digital consent.
