Week 7 April 2026

Audio

For the audio part of my visual essay, as stated in my production plan, I had chosen to personally speak through a script I had written about how work in the digital world is being exploited as free labour. This was to be put on top of background clips which synced up with some of my words in the essay.

I recorded the narration audio with my phone's microphone. At first i thought this would be substantial but upon listening back, I realised how flat and unfamiliar it sounded to have just voice and video.

To resolve this, I chose to incorporate background music underneath the vocals which would fill some of the silences and pauses. This is common practice in visual essays as following a neuroscience study (Kwon, Lee and Lee, 2022), it was found that background music beneath a film helps to retain human focus and interest in what they are watching.

I searched for “robotic” in Pixabay’s music library, aiming to fill the empty space with something dystopian and unsettling. That’s when I came across Robotic Dreams by DSTechnician. The choice of a dystopian, mysterious track was intentional—it echoes the broader question at the heart of the piece: what actually is work in the digital world? Do we really understand it? Is anyone fully aware of the systems of exploitation at play? And what does the future hold for our data as it becomes increasingly monetised? This track captures that ambiguity perfectly, reinforcing the sense of unease and uncertainty that underpins these questions.
