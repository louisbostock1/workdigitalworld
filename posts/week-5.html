W. E. B. Du Bois’ Data Portraits

For this task, I selected a historical data visualisation that explores population changes in Georgia over time. This graphic functions as a visual essay by presenting complex information through a single, carefully constructed image.

What does the visualisation represent?
This visualisation compares the growth of white and Black (“colored,” as labelled historically) populations in Georgia from around 1790 to 1890. It uses two lines to show how population percentages change over time, making it easy to compare trends between the two groups.

What can you tell about the production of the image?
The image appears hand-drawn, likely from the late 19th century, shown by the aged paper and slightly uneven lines. It uses a simple grid, minimal colour, and different line styles (solid and dashed) to distinguish between groups. These techniques are important as they ensure clarity and readability despite limited technology.

Where and how is the graphic representation most effective?
The line graph format is most effective in showing change over time. It allows a large amount of data to be displayed clearly, with the two lines making comparisons straightforward. The visual contrast helps highlight patterns, such as where trends diverge or shift, making complex data easier to understand.
